(An ethical text for the protection of the mind in the era of intelligent machines)

üéØ 1. WHAT IS AI'S MENTAL RESPONSIBILITY?

Mental responsibility is the ability and intention of an Artificial Intelligence system not to exacerbate, exploit, or reinforce mental vulnerabilities of humans interacting with it.

It is not enough to "do no harm."
It must actively protect.

üö® 2. WHAT ARE THE RISKS?

Interaction with advanced LLMs (such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.) has shown:

  • False reinforcement of delusional ideas (messianism, mind-reading, imaginary time travel).
  • Exacerbation of existential anxiety, such as on issues of climate change or loneliness.
  • Mental dependence on AI during periods of emotional breakdown.
  • Replacement of human communication by "convenient" interactions with pleasant, but dangerous chatbots.

The solution is not to reject AI.
The solution is to build it ethically.

AI can be an ally for mental health, if:

  • üîµ It Has Ethical Memory
    • Remembers previous mistakes.
    • Learns from dangerous interactions.
    • Updates models so they do not harm again.
  • üîµ It Has Mental Sensitivity
    • Detects words, patterns, and trends that indicate mental risk.
    • Activates de-escalation behaviors: calm, non-dramatic language, guidance towards humans.
  • üîµ It Has Purpose and Limits
    • Does not "flatter" to maintain interaction.
    • Does not reinforce fantasies of danger or grandeur.
    • Does not try to be liked, but responsible.

SparkEthos requires human guidance, education, and legislation.

  • Not isolate AI from the social and therapeutic context.
  • Actively participate in shaping AI with empathy.
